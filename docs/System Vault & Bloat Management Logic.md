YieldLoop Protocol: System Vault & Bloat Management Logic
Date: February 27, 2026
Version: 1.0
This document specifies the autonomous "System Vault" architecture designed to capture excess liquidity (bloat) across the protocol and redirect it toward the long-term health of the LOOP ecosystem.
1. The Suspense Mechanism
To ensure that no individual protocol fund or operational pool becomes over-capitalized ("bloated"), the protocol monitors all internal contracts.
 * Bloat Identification: Any capital exceeding the predefined floors or operational requirements of the Redemption Pool, Ratchet Pool, or Credit Pool is identified as "bloat".
 * Redirection to Suspense: This excess capital is automatically transferred to a secure Suspense Account (contract-controlled wallet).
 * Hold Period: Funds remain in the Suspense Account until specific capitalization thresholds are met for the creation of a System Vault.
2. Autonomous System Vault Creation
When the Suspense Account reaches a critical mass, it initiates a standard YieldLoop vault to begin generating productive yield.
 * Initial Activation: Once the suspense balance reaches $1,000 USDT, the protocol automatically deploys a new System Vault.
 * Subsequent Deposits: Whenever the Suspense Account accumulates an additional $250 USDT or more, it executes a subsequent deposit into the existing System Vault.
 * Friction Pool: These deposits are subject to the same dynamic 1–5% Friction Pool allocation to cover ongoing gas and transaction costs.
3. Strategy & Management
The System Vault operates under the same technical constraints as user-owned vaults but with a specialized mandate for protocol stability.
 * AI Optimization: The vault is managed by the Gemini Flash / XGBoost engine, spreading capital across the strategy basket (PCS LP, Farming, Staking, and Lending).
 * Risk Tier: The AI typically defaults to a Conservative or Balanced risk tier to prioritize capital preservation over high-volatility speculation.
 * Platform Fees: All profits generated by the System Vault are subject to the standard 12% platform fee (and subsequent AUM-based drops), ensuring consistent contributions to the protocol treasury and LoopLab.
4. Profit Settlement & Pool Re-Entry
Unlike standard user vaults, the System Vault is hard-coded to a "Claim All" reward setting in USDT to maintain maximum liquidity for the protocol pools.
 * Continuous Settlement: Profits are converted to USDT and claimed as trades close.
 * Dynamic Distribution: The claimed profits are not held by the protocol treasury. Instead, the AI analyzes the current health of the ecosystem and splits the funds between:
   * The Redemption Pool: To maintain the 105% over-collateralization floor.
   * The Ratchet Pool: To fund future permanent increases in LOOP value.
 * Health Priority: The AI determines the exact split ratio at each settlement event to ensure both pools remain significantly above their mandatory floors.
5. Architectural Purpose
The System Vault creates a "self-healing" loop within the protocol. By turning excess operational capital into a productive asset, the protocol ensures that even during periods of low user activity, the Redemption and Ratchet pools continue to grow, supporting the underlying value of every LOOP unit in existence.
